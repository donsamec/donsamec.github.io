The Problem You're Facing

Modern developments are beautiful. But they often feel cold, unwelcoming, and forgettable.

Residential Properties

Tenants pass through lobbies without engaging. Your space doesn't feel like home—it feels like a hotel.

Corporate Offices

Employees spend 8 hours in sterile environments. Retention suffers. Culture feels forced.

Hotels & Hospitality

Guests crave authentic, local experiences. Generic decor doesn't drive bookings or social shares.

Healthcare Spaces

Waiting rooms amplify anxiety. Clinical environments add stress when people need calm.

The Solution: Material Memory

I create site-specific installations using layered, reclaimed materials that give spaces a "human heartbeat."

This isn't decoration. It's placemaking as infrastructure—strategically designed to solve psychological problems:

  • Mitigate architectural sterility in modern developments
  • Create natural gathering points that encourage interaction
  • Signal belonging and authenticity over exclusivity
  • Transform pass-through zones into destinations

The result? Spaces where people slow down, engage, and feel at home.

Rooted in Cork

For the past five years, Cork has been my home, my studio, and my endless source of inspiration. I walk this city constantly—camera in hand, eyes scanning textures, fragments, moments of beauty hiding in plain sight.

Every photograph I capture on these walks, every urban abstract, every street scene becomes raw material for the work I create. Cork's energy, its vibrancy, its flux—all of it gets woven into the installations I make.

When you commission work from me, you're not just getting art. You're getting a piece of Cork itself—its character, its memory, its spirit embedded into your space.

Residents don't just want beautiful spaces. They want to feel connected to where they live. Employees don't just want offices. They want to work somewhere that feels like somewhere.

That's what Cork gives us. And that's what I give back through every piece I create.
